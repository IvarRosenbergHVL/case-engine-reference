# Terminology

This document defines core terms used throughout CER.

## Case

A **case** is a structured investigative experience built from hidden truth, evidence, documents, and player discovery.

A case is not a linear story.

## Truth

**Truth** is the complete objective state of the case universe.

Truth exists whether or not any document describes it.

## Fact

A **fact** is an atomic statement that is objectively true within the case universe.

Example:

```text
A person entered a room at a specific time.
```

## Claim

A **claim** is something asserted by a person, document, system, or source.

A claim may be true, false, partial, mistaken, biased, or unknown.

## Evidence

**Evidence** is information that supports, contradicts, contextualizes, or complicates one or more facts or claims.

Evidence is not always reliable.

## Document

A **document** is a representation of one or more evidence fragments.

A document MUST NOT be treated as objective truth.

## Player-facing document

A player-facing document is included in the package given to players.

It MUST NOT reveal facilitator-only truth directly.

## Facilitator material

Facilitator material contains solution, hints, confirmed facts, likely inferences, uncertainties, and guidance for hosted play.

## Discovery

**Discovery** is the changing understanding of the players over time.

Discovery is modeled separately from truth.

## Red herring

A red herring is a misleading but plausible information structure that diverts attention from the correct interpretation.

It SHOULD be meaningful and explainable, not random.

## Means, motive, opportunity

- **Means**: practical ability to perform the act.
- **Motive**: perceived or objective reason to perform the act.
- **Opportunity**: access, timing, and situational possibility.

A strong case SHOULD balance these across multiple suspects.
