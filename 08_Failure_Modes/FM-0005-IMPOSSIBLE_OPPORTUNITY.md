# FM-0005 — Impossible Opportunity

## Description

Impossible Opportunity occurs when the intended responsible actor cannot reasonably have performed the decisive action under the case's own timeline, location, access, or capability constraints.

## Player impact

Players who reason carefully may correctly reject the intended solution.

The case then becomes unfair because the official answer contradicts the evidence available to players.

## Detection signals

- the intended actor is placed elsewhere at the required time
- travel time is insufficient
- access to a required location is not supported
- a required object is unavailable to the actor
- a witness, record, or document unintentionally eliminates the intended actor
- the actor lacks an established capability that the case requires
- opportunity evidence exists for another actor but not the intended actor

## Common causes

- motive planned before opportunity
- late timeline edits
- alibi documents added without rechecking the solution
- location constraints ignored
- document realism introducing unintended elimination
- evidence distribution emphasizing the wrong actor

## Repair strategies

- add fair opportunity support for the intended actor
- adjust timeline, access, or location constraints
- revise or clarify alibi evidence
- change the intended responsible actor only as a major truth repair
- add evidence explaining how opportunity existed
- revalidate discovery and elimination paths

## Severity

Critical when it affects the final solution.

A case SHOULD NOT be released if its intended solution requires impossible opportunity.
