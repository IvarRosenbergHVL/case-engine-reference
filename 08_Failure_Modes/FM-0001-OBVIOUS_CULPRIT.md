# FM-0001 — Obvious Culprit

## Description

The intended culprit becomes uniquely supported too early, making the remainder of the archive feel like confirmation rather than investigation.

## Player impact

Players may solve the case prematurely.

Discussion becomes narrower, wrong hypotheses feel artificial, and later documents lose value.

## Detection signals

A validator or reviewer may detect this when:

- one suspect has strong means, motive, and opportunity evidence early
- other suspects lack plausible support
- suspicion does not shift meaningfully during play
- major red herrings are weak or easy to dismiss
- early documents contain too much solution-critical information

## Common causes

- uneven suspect design
- overpowered early evidence
- insufficient plausible wrong hypotheses
- no meaningful suspect balance
- a single document exposes too many critical facts
- red herrings without evidence support

## Repair strategies

Possible repairs include:

- strengthen at least one plausible wrong hypothesis
- move strong culprit evidence later or lower its visibility
- add fair suspicion support for other suspects
- split solution evidence across more documents
- add an elimination path that occurs later
- reduce explicitness of early evidence exposures

## Severity

Usually Major.

Can be Critical if the case is marketed as medium or hard difficulty.
