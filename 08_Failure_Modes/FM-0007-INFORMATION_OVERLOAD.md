# FM-0007 — Information Overload

## Description

Information Overload occurs when the case contains so much undifferentiated material that players cannot reasonably identify what matters, compare evidence, or maintain a working theory.

## Player impact

Players may stop investigating and instead skim randomly, guess, or rely on facilitator hints.

The difficulty comes from volume and cognitive fatigue rather than meaningful reasoning.

## Detection signals

- many documents have no clear role
- repeated information appears without adding discovery value
- critical clues are buried among similar low-value details
- players cannot summarize what they know after reading
- playtime is consumed by sorting rather than reasoning
- clue density varies wildly between documents
- red herrings outnumber useful investigative paths

## Common causes

- adding documents to increase perceived package size
- preserving too much generated text
- lack of document role planning
- insufficient document distribution review
- confusing realism with volume
- no information density target

## Repair strategies

- remove documents without clear role
- merge redundant documents
- reduce repeated low-value detail
- improve document metadata and headings
- add better signposting through natural in-world structure
- rebalance clue density
- strengthen discovery paths

## Severity

Medium to high.

Information overload is high severity when it prevents fair discovery within the intended playtime.
