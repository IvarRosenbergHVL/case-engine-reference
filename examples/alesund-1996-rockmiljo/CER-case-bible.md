# Tre steder å dø — Ålesund 1996 rockemiljø case bible

This example is a CER-oriented case bible for a fictional document-based cold case set in Ålesund in 1996. It is intended as a working example for validating whether a case concept is ready to become player-facing documents.

The case uses real place context as environmental grounding, but all suspects, victim, incriminating actions, crimes, relationships, dialogue, and case-specific events are fictional unless explicitly marked as historical context.

## CER positioning

- Case type: document-based homicide investigation
- Package mode: static archive, print-and-play
- Player goal: identify the killer, method, location, motive, and cover-up mechanism
- Intended solution model: means + motive + opportunity + contradiction chain
- Difficulty target: medium/advanced
- Core risk: many plausible suspects and rich social context may overload players unless the evidence graph and discovery graph are explicit before document generation

## Real-world contextual anchors

The case should use these as setting anchors only:

- Zipper, Tollbugata 2: new rock/sports pub and concert venue in the 1996 case frame.
- Pizzaen, Løvenvoldgata: established rock pub/social hub in the case frame.
- Smutthullet Pub: newly opened social node in the 1996 case frame.
- Ålesund center: short walking distances, nightlife movement, harbor weather, late autumn darkness, and small-scene social overlap.

Do not make real people suspects, criminals, liars, or fictional witnesses. If a real local figure is referenced, use only respectful environmental/historical context. Use fictional staff and witnesses for all case mechanics.

## Hidden truth graph summary

### Victim

Martin "Molo" Røsvik, 27, vocalist/guitarist/lyricist in Molo & Måne.

### Killer

Jonas Kippervik, 33, sound technician, former guitarist, unofficial producer and technical enabler for Molo & Måne.

### True homicide event

- Date: night of 16–17 November 1996
- True death window: approximately 01:18–01:24
- Location: back/storage room at Zipper, Tollbugata 2
- Weapon: heavy base from a microphone stand
- Method: single forceful blow to the temple during confrontation; victim falls into/near equipment case
- Immediate cover-up: Jonas stages scene as drunken fall during post-gig equipment handling; removes DAT master; disrupts cable/monitor layout

### Motive stack

1. Credit and authorship: Martin planned to take songs and production sound forward without crediting Jonas.
2. Master control: Martin intended to bring the DAT master to Bergen the next day.
3. Public humiliation: Martin reduced Jonas to a technician in front of people who mattered to Jonas.
4. Identity collapse: Jonas saw Martin's possible breakthrough as another instance where he would remain invisible while others used his work.

### Why case stayed unresolved

The original investigation failed not because Jonas built a perfect plan, but because many witnesses concealed smaller, unrelated truths:

- Wenche concealed poor key control at Zipper.
- Silje concealed that she had taken contract papers from Martin's jacket to read them.
- Kai concealed unauthorized live recordings.
- Morten concealed that the record interest was only an informal letter of intent.
- Ragna concealed her plan to leave the band.
- Eivind concealed part of his route and a personal meeting.
- Nikko concealed that he saw Martin and Jonas leave Smutthullet at different times.
- Arild provided an imprecise protective alibi for Jonas.

## Core actors

### A01 — Martin "Molo" Røsvik

- Role: victim; front figure of Molo & Måne
- Public face: charismatic, ambitious, poetic, restless
- Private reality: insecure about being trapped locally; willing to exaggerate outside interest
- Relationship to case: caused several conflicts through selective truth-telling around the Bergen contact, song credits, and master recording
- Victim clue value: his documents show the motive field, especially the absence of Jonas from credit notes

### A02 — Jonas Kippervik

- Role: killer; sound technician; unofficial producer
- Public face: competent, demanding, somewhat bitter, practical
- Private reality: sees himself as musician and co-creator, not service staff
- Motive: erased creative contribution, threatened loss of master, humiliation
- Opportunity: had Zipper access, technical reason to be near back room, knew equipment
- Means: handled microphone stands and rigging; knew how to disrupt scene plausibly
- Lies: claims he was too drunk/uncertain and did not handle the stand after the show; claims he did not follow Martin from Smutthullet

### A03 — Ragna Vik

- Role: bassist; musical stabilizer
- Motivation field: credit conflict; band ownership; exhaustion from administrative labor
- False suspicion: writes angry note "Ikkje la han ta Måne" and lies about returning to Zipper
- Hidden truth: returned for bass equipment and saw Jonas exit the back area, but did not understand its significance
- Document value: exposes authorship conflict and gives a partial corroborating observation

### A04 — Eivind "Evo" Sæther

- Role: drummer
- Motivation field: unpaid debt, public argument, fear of being left behind
- False suspicion: injured knuckle, temper, physical conflict stereotype
- Hidden truth: took a route via a private meeting and lied to keep that person out of the case
- Document value: strong red herring; later eliminable through timeline and independent witness support

### A05 — Nils "Nikko" Giske

- Role: guitarist; Martin's old friend
- Motivation field: emotional abandonment, possible replacement before studio work
- False suspicion: missing alibi; guitar pick near scene
- Hidden truth: went to Silje, later saw Martin and Jonas leave Smutthullet separately, and failed to intervene
- Document value: emotional red herring and eventual opportunity corroboration

### A06 — Silje Alnes

- Role: photographer; Martin's ex-partner
- Motivation field: personal betrayal, image rights, continued attachment
- False suspicion: had access to Martin's jacket and hid contact sheets
- Hidden truth: took contract papers to read them, then replaced them; concealed photos out of fear
- Document value: contact sheets connect Zipper, Pizzaen, Smutthullet, Martin, Jonas, and the brown envelope

### A07 — Morten Dale

- Role: Bergen music contact
- Motivation field: wanted Martin as marketable front figure; not the whole band democracy
- False suspicion: secret meeting and misrepresented agreement
- Hidden truth: only had informal interest/letter of intent; wanted master copy but not homicide
- Document value: reveals Martin exaggerated breakthrough and triggered conflicts

### A08 — Wenche Løvik

- Role: fictional Zipper manager/duty responsible
- Motivation field: protect venue from key-control/scanning/license trouble
- False suspicion: changes statement about locked rooms and access
- Hidden truth: key routine failed; Jonas retained access longer than he should
- Document value: opportunity/access proof

### A09 — Kai Hovden

- Role: fanzine writer and cassette recorder
- Motivation field: conflict over unauthorized recordings; social marginality
- False suspicion: concealed, edited, and sold recordings
- Hidden truth: accidentally captured the decisive pre-homicide conversation at Smutthullet
- Document value: key audio transcript exposure; unreliable but corroboratable

### A10 — Grete Standal

- Role: fictional bartender at Smutthullet
- Motivation field: neutral observer; no deep loyalty to band
- False suspicion: late recovered memory may be doubted
- Hidden truth: remembers Jonas drinking coffee and leaving shortly after Martin
- Document value: timeline precision and sobriety contradiction

### A11 — Arild Moen

- Role: former bandmate of Jonas
- Motivation field: loyalty to Jonas; old riff dispute; discomfort with younger band politics
- False suspicion: old demo creates broader song-theft motive
- Hidden truth: gave vague alibi because he did not want to imagine Jonas capable of violence
- Document value: explains Jonas' psychology and provides a false alibi that can be decomposed

## Critical facts

CF01: Martin was alive at Smutthullet around 01:05 and left before Jonas.

CF02: Jonas followed Martin within a few minutes, despite claiming uncertainty or non-memory.

CF03: Jonas had access to Zipper/back room and reason to be there.

CF04: The true death window is later than the original police assumption.

CF05: The DAT master and song/production credits were active conflicts that night.

CF06: The weapon was consistent with stage equipment Jonas handled.

CF07: The staged fall explanation conflicts with wound mechanics and equipment placement.

CF08: Other suspects have motives/lies, but their opportunity or means fails under timeline validation.

CF09: Jonas' claimed impairment is contradicted by coffee receipt and technical behavior.

CF10: Independent exposures converge: audio, bar receipt, contact sheet, key list, rig list, physical evidence.

## Discovery graph

### DN01 — The original timeline is wrong

Players compare original police assumptions with Smutthullet evidence and realize Martin lived later than assumed.

- Supports: E01, E02, E04
- Required for: opportunity analysis
- Risk: bottleneck if only audio exposes it
- Repair: ensure at least two independent documents show Martin at Smutthullet around 01:05

### DN02 — The case has three social locations, not one crime scene

Players understand movement between Zipper, Pizzaen, and Smutthullet.

- Supports: map, photos, receipts, witness statements
- Required for: timeline and suspect elimination
- Risk: information overload
- Repair: include a one-page location/timeline worksheet or police map with handwritten annotations

### DN03 — Many people lied, but not for the same reason

Players learn that lies are not equal to murder.

- Supports: all suspect statements and contradiction notes
- Required for: fair red herring handling
- Risk: obvious culprit if all non-Jonas lies are too weak
- Repair: give Ragna, Eivind, Silje, Morten, and Nikko meaningful but non-murder secrets

### DN04 — Jonas had the strongest combined means, motive, and opportunity

Players assemble access, weapon handling, motive, and movement.

- Supports: E01-E10
- Required for: solution
- Risk: too obvious if Jonas is hostile in every document
- Repair: make Jonas' early documents competent and credible; hide violence behind technical concern

### DN05 — The staged fall does not fit the physical evidence

Players reject accident theory.

- Supports: E07, E08, E06
- Required for: homicide conclusion
- Risk: too forensic-heavy
- Repair: provide plain-language forensic note and scene sketch that normal players can interpret

### DN06 — Red herrings can be eliminated through independent constraints

Players reject Eivind, Ragna, Silje, Morten, Nikko, Arild, Kai.

- Supports: alibi fragments, motive context, contradictions with opportunity
- Required for: confidence
- Risk: too many suspects
- Repair: suspect matrix in facilitator solution, not player-facing unless desired as hint

## CER validation pass

### Truth validation

Result: warning-pass.

Strengths:

- The objective truth is specific: killer, time, location, method, motive, and staging are defined.
- Non-killer secrets explain why original evidence was muddy.

Warnings:

- Death date must be fixed consistently across all documents.
- If real venue opening dates are used, internal timeline must match the selected month.
- Need precise walking-time assumptions between Zipper, Pizzaen, and Smutthullet.

Repair:

- Freeze canonical date as 16–17 November 1996 unless research proves it impossible.
- Add case constants file with date, venue labels, fictional/nonfictional boundary, and location coordinates/approximate walking times.

### Timeline validation

Result: warning.

Strengths:

- Current murder window is playable and narrow.
- Three-location movement creates a good opportunity puzzle.

Warnings:

- 01:10 Martin leaves / 01:13 Jonas leaves / 01:18-01:24 murder requires plausible walking and entry time.
- 02:18 discovery leaves a long unattended body window; document why cleanup/discovery delay is plausible.

Repair:

- Add a route map and timed movements.
- Decide whether Jonas returned to Smutthullet, Pizzaen, or neither after the murder; all statements must align.

### Evidence validation

Result: pass with bottleneck warning.

Strengths:

- Critical facts have multiple exposures.
- Evidence types vary: audio, photo, receipt, access log, rig list, forensic note, interviews.

Warnings:

- Audio transcript could become too decisive if not corroborated.
- Physical evidence needs enough specificity to defeat accident theory without requiring expert knowledge.

Repair:

- Ensure each critical fact has at least two independent exposures.
- Provide forensic clue in both technical autopsy language and plain police summary.

### Discovery validation

Result: warning-pass.

Strengths:

- Major discovery nodes exist.
- Wrong hypotheses are meaningful and eliminable.

Warnings:

- Players may over-focus on character drama and miss physical staging.
- Too many secrets can blur the distinction between guilt and shame.

Repair:

- Include a facilitator-only suspect matrix and optional hint ladder.
- Make player documents point repeatedly to the question: who could leave Smutthullet, access Zipper, and return/vanish within the window?

### Information economy validation

Result: moderate warning.

Strengths:

- Rich, realistic social world supports immersion.
- Documents have clear roles.

Warnings:

- 10+ actors and three venues is cognitively heavy for print-and-play.
- Red herrings are numerous; without careful document design, players may feel everything matters equally.

Repair:

- Use a case board sheet with suspect names, venues, and time blocks.
- Keep most documents one page.
- Repeat only critical location/time data, not every social detail.

## Release readiness verdict

The Ålesund 1996 case is not yet render-ready, but it is a strong CER-compatible concept. It should proceed to a structured case model and document specifications before prose documents are generated.

Recommended next step:

1. Freeze canonical case constants.
2. Create structured actor, event, evidence, claim, and document IDs.
3. Generate document specs before writing documents.
4. Run validation against critical facts and failure modes.
5. Only then render interviews, reports, photos, correspondence, and media transcripts.

## CER adequacy audit from this example

### What CER handles well

- It correctly forces separation between hidden truth and player-facing documents.
- It pushes the case away from pure story and toward an evidence network.
- The validation layers cover the right concerns: truth, timeline, evidence, documents, discovery, information economy, failure modes, rendering, and packaging.
- Failure modes are directly useful for diagnosing this case.
- Discovery graph thinking is essential for preventing a rich social case from becoming just a pile of atmospheric documents.

### Gaps or improvement opportunities in CER

1. Character realism quality gate

CER has relationship and perception models, but it should more explicitly validate whether suspects are psychologically plausible rather than caricatured. Add a quality dimension for human realism:

- each major actor has ordinary life context
- each lie has a non-murder reason
- each motive has emotional and practical layers
- no suspect is defined only by a trope

2. Venue/social ecology model

This case depends on social spaces: Zipper, Pizzaen, Smutthullet. CER's Location Model should explicitly support social function, not just geometry.

Suggested fields:

- location_role: crime_scene, social_hub, transition_space, false_focus
- social_meaning: old_scene, new_scene, neutral_ground
- access_model: public, staff-only, key-controlled, informal
- witness_density: low/medium/high
- noise_profile: music, alcohol, crowd, weather, closing routines

3. Real-world grounding and fiction boundary

CER should include a safety/legal/ethics rule for cases using real places and local history:

- real people should not be assigned fictional crimes, secrets, or defamatory behavior
- real venues can be environmental anchors
- fictionalized active witnesses should be clearly fictional
- local historical claims should be source-checked and separated from hidden truth

4. Red herring fairness scoring

CER identifies red herrings, but this example shows a need for scoring red herrings by fairness:

- Does the red herring have a legitimate reason to appear suspicious?
- Is there a clear path to eliminate it?
- Does it teach a useful distinction, or only waste time?
- Is it supported by evidence without becoming more plausible than the true solution?

5. Multi-suspect cognitive load thresholds

CER should define stronger heuristics for actor count vs document count vs expected playtime. Ten actors is possible, but only if player aids are included.

Suggested validator warning:

- If actors > 8 and venues > 2, require a map/timeline/suspect aid unless product difficulty is expert.

6. Case constants requirement

CER should require a canonical constants layer before document generation:

- exact date/time
- locations and distances
- actor ages/roles
- death window
- weather/lighting assumptions
- source-of-truth spelling for names and venues

This would prevent continuity drift between generated documents.

7. Document role enforcement

CER says documents expose evidence, but the validator should flag documents without a defined role:

- evidence-bearing
- context-setting
- misdirection
- corroboration
- elimination
- atmosphere-only

Atmosphere-only documents should have a strict budget.

## Proposed CER additions from this audit

- Add a Character Realism validation rule.
- Add Venue Social Ecology to Location Model.
- Add Real-World Grounding and Fiction Boundary rule under Compliance or Artifact Production.
- Add Red Herring Fairness rubric.
- Add Multi-Suspect Cognitive Load heuristic.
- Add Case Constants model as a required pre-document artifact.
- Add Document Role Budget validation.
