# Information Density

Information Density describes how much meaningful information is packed into a document, page, section, or artifact.

## Purpose

A document can be too empty, too dense, or well-balanced.

Information Density helps designers control pacing, readability, clue discovery, and player workload.

## Definition

Information Density is the amount of meaningful information per unit of player material.

Meaningful information includes:

- evidence exposures
- claims
- contradictions
- context clues
- relationship information
- timeline anchors
- suspect information
- elimination support
- atmospheric or procedural information that supports realism

## Density levels

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| Sparse | Mostly atmosphere or simple framing. |
| Light | One or two relevant points. |
| Moderate | Several relevant points with manageable noise. |
| Dense | Many relevant points requiring careful reading. |
| Very dense | High concentration of clues, claims, and dependencies. |

## Good density

Good density depends on document role.

An anchor document may be moderately dense.

A lab report may be dense but structured.

A personal note may be sparse but highly meaningful.

A facilitator solution may be very dense because it is not part of discovery play.

## Density risks

| Risk | Description |
|---|---|
| Underfeeding | Players lack enough information to reason. |
| Overloading | Players cannot separate useful information from volume. |
| False importance | Dense irrelevant material looks more important than it is. |
| Hidden critical clue | A required clue is buried in excessive density. |
| Single-document solution | Too much critical information appears in one artifact. |

## Normative requirements

Critical clues SHOULD NOT be hidden only by excessive density.

A document's density SHOULD match its role and intended difficulty.

A case package SHOULD include varied density across document types.

Very dense documents SHOULD be structured enough to support player use.

## Validation questions

- How many meaningful information units does this document contain?
- Does the density match document role?
- Are critical clues discoverable within the density?
- Does this document contain too much of the solution?
- Does the overall package feel empty, balanced, or overloaded?
